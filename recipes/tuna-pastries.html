Tuna Pastries

Classic tuna pastries, not quite the same recipe that mum makes, but I was lazy and didn't want to head downstairs and ask her about it so this is the closest one I could find online

Filling ingredients

  1. 2 cans of tuna
  2. 2 green onions (chopped)
  3. 2 large cloves of garlic (finely minced)
  4. 2 green chillies (chopped)
  5. 1/2 teaspon turmeric powder
  6. 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
  7. 1 teaspon chilli powder
  8. 1 tablespoon breadcrumbs
  9. A dash of lime juice
  10. Some freshly cracked black pepper
  11. A few coriander leaves (chopped)
  12. 2 tablespoons of butter

    Filling method

  1. Heat butter in a pan and sau te green onions with garlic.
  2. When soft and fragrant, add green chillies along with the spice powders.
  3. Cook on medium heat, fry well and incorporate only the tuna after draining out the brine.
  4. Sprinkle lime juice, freshly cracked black pepper and stir well until combined.
  5. Mix in the breadcrumbs to absorb any moisture.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  7. Set filling aside to cool.

    Pastry ingredients


  1. 1 box of Tenderflake puff pastry (397 grams containg two blocks - available in Canada, how relevant) or frozen puff pastry sheets if you live anywhere else (defrosted before use)

    Pastry Method


  1. Preheat oven to 200C
  2. On a lightly floured surface, roll each block of puff pastry into 9"x9" square.
  3. Cut this large square into 4 equal squares.
  4. Put spoonfuls of filling in each. Brush edges with egg, bring the outer corner to the centre and seal.
  5. With the tines of a fork, (the flat back part of the prongs) mark along the edge of the pastry.
  6. Place each pastry puff in a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  7. Brush the top with egg wash and bake until puffed and brown for 20 minutes.
  8. Serve warm
